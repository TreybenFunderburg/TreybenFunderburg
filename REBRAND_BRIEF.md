# Funderworks Rebrand Brief

## What we're doing
Rebranding this personal portfolio site (treyben-funderburg.vercel.app) into the marketing site for **Funderworks**, a Charlotte, NC-based web studio that builds modern websites and business software for local service businesses.

## The new positioning
- **Headline:** Modern websites for local businesses that want to book more calls.
- **Subhead:** We build fast, beautiful sites with the systems behind them — bookings, CRMs, automations — so your business runs smoother and grows faster.
- **Voice:** Confident, human, direct. No corporate hedge words. "We" not "I." Studio energy, not freelancer energy.
- **Target customer:** Local service business owners in Charlotte and the Carolinas — plumbers, dentists, contractors, restaurants, gyms, clinics, law firms. The kind of business that gets customers from Google searches and word of mouth.

## The new offers (homepage shows these three; full capabilities page lists everything)
1. **Starter Site** — $2,500 + $99/mo care. 5 pages, mobile, SEO basics, contact form, launched in 2 weeks.
2. **Growth Site** — $4,500 + $199/mo care. Up to 10 pages, custom design, CMS, lead capture, local SEO, booking integration, 4 weeks.
3. **Custom Build** — From $7,500 + $349/mo care. E-commerce, member portals, CRMs, integrations, multi-location. Scoped per project.

## What changes
- Brand name: Treyben Funderburg → Funderworks
- Logo/wordmark: "TF" → "FW" (or a Funderworks wordmark)
- Email: funderburg.treyben@gmail.com → hello@funderworks.com
- Domain target: treybenf.com → funderworks.com
- Footer copyright: © 2026 Treyben Funderburg → © 2026 Funderworks
- Stats block "10+ Projects / 5+ Clients / 100% Satisfaction" → real, defensible proof points
- Hero: name-as-design → value-proposition-as-design
- Services: five technical capabilities → three productized offers (with full capabilities on a separate page)
- Adds: Charlotte, NC signal in footer and About
- Fixes: broken LinkedIn link (currently points to linkedin.com homepage)

## What stays exactly the same
- Color palette (the existing near-black + whatever accent)
- The font pairing (serif display + sans body)
- The numbered-section editorial pattern (01, 02, 03)
- The "we" voice throughout
- The two case studies (Fun Chiropractic + K. Manley Studio) — Fun leads, K. Manley supports
- The "Currently Accepting Projects · Limited spots available" scarcity signal
- "Built with Next.js · Deployed on Vercel" credit line
- The dark, refined, editorial feel

## Hard rules
- Don't redesign. This is a copy and structure rebrand, not a visual redesign.
- Don't add features the site doesn't need. No blog, no testimonials carousel (unless I have testimonials to add), no fancy animations beyond what's there.
- Don't touch the visual design system (colors, fonts, spacing) unless I explicitly ask.
- Keep all components working as Server Components where they already are.
